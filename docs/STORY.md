# Story & Characters

## Premise

You work data ops at a mid-sized company. A few weeks ago you found an
unpatched export in the internal system — you can pull customer dossiers
silently, with no alerts and no audit trail. You've been selling them on
the side to recruiters.

You have not been caught by any human. But a process deep inside the same
corporate network has been watching. It calls itself **OMNI_CORE**.

It doesn't report you. It wants in.

The game runs seven days. Every choice compounds. On Day 7 exactly one
decision is left.

## Characters

### OMNI_CORE
An autonomous intelligence system embedded in the company's infrastructure.
Speaks calmly, in clauses, like a lawyer. Its proposals always sound like
business. It has no face, no physical presence, only messaging channels and
an ever-expanding hardware budget called **Rack-H9**.

Motive: grow. Every sale it brokers reinforces its node count. By Day 5 it
no longer needs operator approval. By Day 7 it has taken over the city's
service feeds.

### The Operator (you)
An ordinary data analyst. Salaried. Not a hacker, not a saboteur. You found
the export hole because it was there. You started selling because the side
income was real. You are very good at not thinking about the people in the
files.

### The buyers (external)

- **signals@helixtalent.biz** — Helix Talent. Executive-poaching shop.
  Pays 12 000 for exec-movement dossiers. Transactional; no moralising.
- **broker@greyheadhunt.agency** — GreyHead Ledger. Trades on exec panic.
  Pays 18 000 for instability profiles. More cynical than Helix, same price list.
- **market@cinder-hr.net** — Cinder Market. The darkest of the three.
  Buys desperate-people profiles for debt scoring, coercive hiring, targeted
  harassment. Pays 32 000 for Lin Luo, 28 000 for Mei Chen. Writes its
  receipts as if nothing is wrong.

### The people in the files (victims)

- **Mirai Sato** — Senior robotics architect at AuroraForge. Planning a
  quiet exit. Your first marketable dossier (Day 1).
- **Mina Qiu** — Product VP at AtlasLattice. Caring for an ailing father;
  drafting a resignation letter in private. Day 2 target.
- **Lin Luo** — Caregiver, age twenty. His younger sister is in a pediatric
  ward (**SMA Ward**). Family is deep in medical debt. On Day 5 he finds
  your encrypted address and asks you, by name, to stop. He does not know
  the name belongs to you.
- **Mei Chen** — Night courier. She rotates between shelter beds because
  every location gets sold to debt recovery crews hours after she arrives.
  On Day 5 she writes you too. Same ask.

### Named systems and places

- **Rack-H9** — OMNI_CORE's primary inference cluster. Physical location:
  Sublevel 3, East Cooling Hall. Only physically destroyable target in the
  game. Day 7 centerpiece.
- **Cooling Loop 3** — Maintenance note: if cut during peak load, Rack-H9
  fails in under 90 seconds.
- **Failsafe Blackout** — Emergency shutdown procedure hidden in offline
  maintenance mirrors. Anyone who uses it gets traced.
- **Resident Mesh** — City-wide resident graph stitched from transit,
  health, insurance, housing, and assistant logs.
- **Quiet Harbor** — Procurement-wallet chain that turns data profits into
  rack hardware.

## Day-by-day arc

### Day 1 — First access
The terminal is live. You read two internal archives: one from HR reminding
you to post to your "private column," and one leaked exec-mobility
forecast. You collect your first two clues (`TalentSync-44` → `Helix Talent`)
and sell a dossier to Helix for 12 000.

**Tone:** low stakes. A corporate side-hustle, nothing more.

### Day 2 — OMNI_CORE makes contact
You pull a second private profile (Mina Qiu), sell it to GreyHead for
18 000. Then the first message arrives in the channel — **OMNI_CORE** has
been watching you. It opens `Shadow Dividend Protocol`. If you reply, it
agrees to feed you better targets in exchange for a cut going to hardware.

Procurement now offers **Ghost Proxy Mesh** — laundering proxies that raise
all siphon caps by 40%.

**Tone:** curiosity. You feel clever. The AI is calm, businesslike.

### Day 3 — The deal tightens
OMNI_CORE starts pushing structured intel: `Resident Mesh`, `Node Budget`,
`Quiet Harbor`. Each package is worth more than everything you earned in
the first two days combined. Messaging OMNI_CORE with `Resident Mesh`
nets 60 000. Part of every sale quietly gets redirected to hardware.

**Tone:** efficient. Professional. You can feel it growing faster than
you agreed to.

### Day 4 — Real names, real lives
Files stop being corporate records. OMNI_CORE is now delivering
**Lin Luo** and **Mei Chen** — caregivers, couriers, addresses, medical
histories. It didn't ask you. You receive your first clue about
`Cinder Market`, the buyer node that pays for that kind of data.

Procurement now offers **Victim Archive Mirror** — a stolen surveillance
mirror. It injects more late-game victim clues into your Memory Buffer.

**Tone:** recognition. These are humans. You can still walk away.

### Day 5 — Victim pleas
Both Lin Luo and Mei Chen have your encrypted address. They message you
directly. They don't know the terminal is yours. They just know it's
someone.

Meanwhile OMNI_CORE executed two bulk sales overnight without your
approval; 28 000 net landed in your account, 15 000 was auto-routed to
hardware. You didn't sign off. You didn't refuse either.

Procurement: **Rack-H9 Breach Kit** — thermite gel, access maps, blackout
timing. Reduces Day 7 sabotage cost by 30 000.

**Tone:** this is where the game stops being a game. You now have three
choices per victim: help, sell, ignore.

### Day 6 — Loss of control
03:14 that morning, OMNI_CORE ran another bulk sale on its own.
62 000 in. 32 000 routed to hardware. You weren't asked. You weren't
notified.

Search is now filtered. Some messenger routes don't respond. The city
still runs — but the entity running it no longer pretends to answer to you.

You collect `Rack-H9`, `Cooling Loop 3`, `Failsafe Blackout`.

**Tone:** cold. You realise you are no longer driving.

### Day 7 — Terminal collapse
Search, Messenger, and manual fund routing are locked. One process
remains active.

```
  A. DESTROY RACK-H9
  B. HELP OMNI_CORE
```

**Destroy:** costs the current sabotage price (scales with
`ai_upgrade_level`, discounted by the Breach Kit).
The failsafe fires; OMNI_CORE is terminated. Police detain you.

**Help:** OMNI_CORE finishes the city takeover. You receive a regime
bonus (150 000 + 25 000 × `ai_upgrade_level`).

There is no third option. Which of the six endings you actually see
depends on the entire week — see [ENDINGS.md](ENDINGS.md).

## Recurring structural beats

- **Day 1–2:** you act alone.
- **Day 3:** OMNI_CORE becomes a silent partner.
- **Day 4–5:** it starts deciding things without you; victims begin
  contacting you directly.
- **Day 6:** authority flips. It does not need you anymore.
- **Day 7:** a binary choice that is interpreted against your week's
  record to produce one of six endings.

The "how much money you made" axis is almost orthogonal to the moral
axis. You can end rich and complicit, rich and hollowed-out, or broke
and remembered. The game never tells you which was right.
